Error from using only the larger signal

Two incoherent RMS pressures add in quadrature ptot = √(p₁² + p₂²). This finds the % error if the smaller signal is ignored and only the larger one is used to estimate the total RMS. Enter the two RMS values, or just the ratio (smaller ÷ larger).

Error = (p₁ − ptot) / ptot = 1/√(1 + r²) − 1,   r = p₂/p₁

Distance Attenuation / Geometric Spreading

Given a level L₁ at distance r₁, find the level L₂ at distance r₂ as the sound spreads out. A point source spreads spherically (−6 dB per doubling); a line source (e.g. a continuous stream of traffic) spreads cylindrically (−3 dB per doubling).

Compute L₂ at a new distance

Point (spherical): L₂ = L₁ − 20·log10(r₂/r₁)  ·  Line (cylindrical): L₂ = L₁ − 10·log10(r₂/r₁)
Optional extra source is energy-combined with L₁ at r₁ first: L₁ ← 10·log10(10L₁/10 + 10L_extra/10)

Solve for distance from two levels

Two SPLs measured along the same line: L₁ at the near point (distance y from the source) and L₂ a further Δr away. Back out the near distance y (e.g. a rifle = point source).

y = Δr / (10ΔL/20 − 1) point  ·  y = Δr / (10ΔL/10 − 1) line  ·  ΔL = L₁−L₂

Duct: Sound Power → Microphone Voltage

A source radiates a known acoustic power as plane waves along a pipe (no reflections / anechoic termination). The wave fills the duct cross-section, so I = W/A with A = πd²/4; for a plane wave prms = √(I·ρc), and a microphone of sensitivity S dB re 1 V/Pa gives V = prms·10S/20 (Units 1–2, 4).

Compute microphone voltage

W = 10⁻¹²·10Lw/10  ·  I = W/A  ·  prms = √(I·ρc)  ·  V = prms·10S/20. Higher-order modes cut on above fc = 1.841·c/(πd).

Loudness — Phons & Sones

The loudness level (phons) of a tone is read from the equal-loudness contour passing through its frequency & SPL. Loudness in sones converts from phons by S = 2(LL − 40)/10 (valid for LL ≥ 40 phon).

Phons → Sones

Sones → Phons

LL = 40 + 10·log2(S)

Reading the equal-loudness contours

For a pure tone, find its frequency on the x-axis and its SPL on the y-axis; the phon value is the label of the contour through that point. A 1 kHz tone has loudness level (phon) equal to its SPL (dB). Example (Quiz 6): a 500 Hz tone at 76 dB lies on the 80 phon contour ⇒ S = 2(80−40)/10 = 16 sones.

Speech Interference Level (PSIL) & Communication

Preferred Speech Interference Level = arithmetic mean of the octave-band SPLs centred at 500, 1000 and 2000 Hz: PSIL = (L500 + L1000 + L2000)/3.

Statistical Levels & SEL

Percentile (LN) and single-event levels describe a fluctuating noise.

What each term means (Quiz 6)

  • L1 — exceeded 1 % of the time → close to the maximum level.
  • L10 — exceeded 10 % → typical of intrusive/peak traffic noise.
  • L90 — exceeded 90 % → the background level.
  • L99 — exceeded 99 % → close to the minimum level.
  • Leq — energy-average; lies between L90 and L1.
  • SEL — Sound Exposure Level; energy of an event referenced to 1 s, so it is the largest of the set.

Ordering (typical): L99 < L90 < Leq < L10 < L1 < SEL.

SEL ↔ Leq

SEL = Leq + 10·log10(T / 1 s)
