Combine / Add Sound Levels

Add incoherent sound levels (dB). Levels combine on an energy basis: Ltot = 10·log10( Σ 10Li/10 ). Use this to add the noise of several sources, to combine octave-band levels into a total, or to find the level of N identical sources. The combined result is also reported as an RMS sound pressure p = 2×10−5·10L/20 Pa.

Increase in level when more sources are added

e.g. a compound contains N₁ barking dogs measured at L₁; add more so there are N₂.

ΔL = 10·log10(N₂ / N₁)  ·  New level = L₁ + ΔL

Equivalent Continuous Level Leq

General form over a reference period T: Leq,T = 10·log10( (1/T) · Σ ti·10Li/10 ). Times may be entered in any unit as long as T uses the same unit.

Leq from levels & durations

One row per noise component: level, duration (e.g. 86, 3.5). Duration is the total time at that level.

Leq from discrete events (e.g. train / vehicle pass-bys)

One row per event type: level, single-event duration, number of events. Set the reference period in the same time unit as the durations.

e.g. 24 h = 86400 s

Leq,T = 10·log10( (1/T)·Σ Ni·ti·10Li/10 )

Occupational Noise Dose & Exposure Limits

Australian OH&S criterion: LAeq,8h = 85 dB(A) with a 3 dB exchange rate.

Max permissible time at LAeq:   T = Tc / 2(LAeq − Lc)/q  ·  Dose = (Σti/Ti) where Ti = Tc/2(Li−Lc)/q

Loudness — Phons & Sones

The loudness level (phons) of a tone is read from the equal-loudness contour passing through its frequency & SPL. Loudness in sones converts from phons by S = 2(LL − 40)/10 (valid for LL ≥ 40 phon).

Phons → Sones

Sones → Phons

LL = 40 + 10·log2(S)

Reading the equal-loudness contours

For a pure tone, find its frequency on the x-axis and its SPL on the y-axis; the phon value is the label of the contour through that point. A 1 kHz tone has loudness level (phon) equal to its SPL (dB). Example (Quiz 6): a 500 Hz tone at 76 dB lies on the 80 phon contour ⇒ S = 2(80−40)/10 = 16 sones.

Speech Interference Level (PSIL) & Communication

Preferred Speech Interference Level = arithmetic mean of the octave-band SPLs centred at 500, 1000 and 2000 Hz: PSIL = (L500 + L1000 + L2000)/3.

Statistical Levels & SEL

Percentile (LN) and single-event levels describe a fluctuating noise.

What each term means (Quiz 6)

  • L1 — exceeded 1 % of the time → close to the maximum level.
  • L10 — exceeded 10 % → typical of intrusive/peak traffic noise.
  • L90 — exceeded 90 % → the background level.
  • L99 — exceeded 99 % → close to the minimum level.
  • Leq — energy-average; lies between L90 and L1.
  • SEL — Sound Exposure Level; energy of an event referenced to 1 s, so it is the largest of the set.

Ordering (typical): L99 < L90 < Leq < L10 < L1 < SEL.

SEL ↔ Leq

SEL = Leq + 10·log10(T / 1 s)

Duct: Sound Power → Microphone Voltage

A source radiates a known acoustic power as plane waves down a pipe (no reflections / anechoic termination). The wave fills the duct cross-section, so the intensity is I = W / A with A = πd²/4. For a plane wave the RMS pressure is p = √(I·ρc), and a microphone of sensitivity S dB re 1 V/Pa gives V = p · 10S/20.

Compute microphone voltage

W = Wref·10LW/10  ·  I = W/A  ·  p = √(I·ρc)  ·  V = p·10S/20. Higher-order modes cut on above fc = 1.841·c/(πd).
